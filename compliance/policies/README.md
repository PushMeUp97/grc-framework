# Corporate Security Policies

## 📋 Overview

This directory contains templates and guidelines for developing comprehensive corporate security policies that form the foundation of an organization's security posture and GRC program.

## 📁 Policy Categories

### Access Control
Policies governing who can access what resources, when, and under what circumstances.

**Topics include:**
- User access provisioning and de-provisioning
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Privileged Access Management (PAM)
- Multi-factor Authentication (MFA) requirements
- Password policies

### Incident Response
Procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

**Topics include:**
- Incident classification and severity levels
- Response team roles and responsibilities
- Investigation procedures
- Communication and escalation protocols
- Post-incident review and lessons learned

### Data Protection
Policies protecting organizational and personal data throughout its lifecycle.

**Topics include:**
- Data classification and handling
- Encryption standards
- Backup and recovery procedures
- Data retention and disposal
- Remote access security

### Asset Management
Controls for managing organizational IT and physical assets.

**Topics include:**
- Hardware and software inventory
- Device management and controls
- Lifecycle management
- Disposal procedures

## 🎯 Best Practices

1. **Tailor to Your Organization**: Customize templates to reflect your specific environment
2. **Regular Reviews**: Update policies annually or when business changes occur
3. **Clear Communication**: Ensure all employees understand relevant policies
4. **Training**: Provide regular security awareness training
5. **Enforcement**: Consistently apply policies and monitor compliance
